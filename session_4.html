Session 4

Intro

Today, your adventure begins in one of the most fascinating ecosystems on Earth: the desert. This is a place where life has learned to survive with very little water and extreme temperatures. You’ll meet creatures that are experts at hiding from the sun, plants that store water like treasure, and learn how everything in this ecosystem is connected.

Your mission is to explore the desert, solve challenges, and collect stamps for your Explorer's Passport. Pay close attention, work together, and discover the secrets of this incredible place. Each challenge will bring you closer to unlocking the final treasure. Are you ready to begin?

 

Today, we are going to see this infographic and talk about how we can preserve our biodiversity. 


Remember! Biodiversity is the amazing variety of all the living things on Earth, like animals, plants, bugs, and even tiny creatures we can't see! It’s like a giant puzzle where every living thing has its own important piece. Biodiversity helps keep nature healthy—plants give us oxygen to breathe, bees help grow our food, and forests provide homes for animals. When we take care of all these living things and their homes, the whole planet stays strong and beautiful!

Biodiversity is important because every living thing is connected, like a big team working together. When one part of nature is harmed—like animals losing their homes or plants disappearing—it can cause problems for the whole team. For example, without bees, many plants wouldn’t grow food for us to eat. Healthy biodiversity helps keep the air clean, the soil rich, and the water fresh. That’s why it’s so important to protect nature and all the creatures that live in it—because when nature is healthy, we are too!

Infografía Comparación Dibujo a Mano Amarillo Naranja y Negro de Carlota Gómez

 

Are you ready? 

Now, it’s your turn to create a poster or infographic of your own. 

In the poster you have to represent one good action and a bad action when talking about preserving biodiversity. So, in groups decide what you are going to represent in the poster. You can use the infographic above as an example. 

Need a bit of inspiration? 

Here are some ideas you can use to base your poster on: deforestation, gas pollution, recycling, planting native trees, using renewable energy, introducing breeding programs for endangered species… Don’t forget that at the end of class you will have to present your project in front of your classmates.
